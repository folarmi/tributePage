Here is a timeline on Professor Achebe's life

  • 1930 - Albert Chinualumogu Achebe was born to Isaac and Janet in the Igbo town of Ogidi in southeastern Nigeria
  • 1948 - Was admitted as a Major Scholar in the university of Ibadan first intake and given a bursary to study medicine
  • He abandoned the study of medicine and changed to English, history, and theology
  • 1954 - Worked with Nigerian Broadcasting Service (NBS)
  • 1958 - Published his widely acclaimed novel Things fall apart
  • 1960 - Awarded the Rockefeller Fellowship
  • 1961 Got married to Christiana Chinwe (Christie) Okoli
  • 1967 - Became a supporter of Biafran independence and acted as ambassador for the people of the new nation
  • 1964 - Published Arrow of God
  • 1990 - Had an accident that made him use the wheelchair for the rest of his life
  • 2007 - Awarded the Man Booker International Prize
  • 2010 - was awarded The Dorothy and Lillian Gish Prize for $300,000
  • 2012 - Published his last Book There Was a Country: A Personal History of Biafra.
  • 2013 - Died in Boston, United States
